## UI Frameworks - one thing we agreed on early was to stay away from the volatile world of UI frameworks. - competition is fierce, look at Sencha Touch, WinJS or jQuery-Mobile - we chose to focus on enabling whichever UI framework you chose, cordova is the container and the bridge - framework7, react, ionic, onsen ui, angular, vue, svelte
## Competitors - not really competitors to cordova, but these folks may or may think of cordova as a competitor - native, react-native, ionic (avacado/ capacitor), > All four technologies allow you to build real native mobile apps for both iOS and Android - without the need to learn Swift, ObjectiveC, Java or Kotlin!

## The next wave of innovators, contributors,
While in the past we had large scale teams coming from Google, IBM, Intel, Microsoft, Blackberry, Adobe, there is currently no major contributor, and the project is managed by a small team of very dedicated volunteers. Over the last few years there has been a decline in the number of active contributors. - Probably because the project has become mature and there is less change needed. - Perhaps because we are not the new shiny project we once were, there is less excitement around having your name tied to the project, it is good enough to say that your company 'did' commit. - App stores have lost their shine, a good mobile experience in a web browser is enough for most businesses. - Maybe, the rise of other web-based workflows like (pwa) have shown enough promise that there is less interest in building apps for the stores.
